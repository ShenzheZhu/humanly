# Humanly Role-Based User Evaluation: Survey Question List

This document maintains the full question list for the Humanly role-based user
evaluation described in the paper. 

## Owner Survey

Survey link:
https://app.deliberation.io/participate/97081bc5-8117-4f16-9036-d8b0603a7470

Setup: participants use the deployed Humanly Publisher Portal to create and
publish a writing task, then return to Deliberation.io to answer the following questions.

### Usability

1. The process of creating a task in Humanly was easy to do.
2. Humanly provided comprehensive controls for configuring AI access, writing
   rules, submission policies, and task timing.
3. From a task publisher's perspective (e.g., professor or instructor),
   Humanly's configuration options seem flexible enough for various writing
   scenarios.
4. I would be comfortable using Humanly to create tasks in the future.

### Role Perception

1. From a task publisher's perspective (e.g., professor or instructor), Humanly
   makes task setup feel transparent because it exposes writing-policy controls
   that are often partially hidden or unavailable in standard online assignment
   platforms.
2. Compared with a typical online assignment platform (e.g., Canvas), Humanly
   would make me more confident that participants are not using unauthorized
   external AI tools.

## Writer Survey

Survey link:
https://app.deliberation.io/participate/6d82d487-99d9-49cb-af6a-802a086f45dc

Setup: participants open an assigned Humanly writing task, write in the deployed
Humanly workspace, generate or submit the requested certificate, then return to
Deliberation.io to answer the following questions.

### Usability

1. The Humanly writing workspace was easy to use, and after this experience, I
   would know how to complete a similar writing task in Humanly in the future.
2. The in-platform AI assistant was useful for completing the writing task.
3. The AI optimization shortcuts inside the editor, such as Fix Grammar, Improve
   Writing, Simplify, Make Formal, and Ask AI, were useful for improving my
   writing.

### Role Perception

1. While writing in Humanly, I felt more accountable for following the task
   rules than I would in a standard online writing platform (e.g., Google Docs).
2. Compared with a standard online writing platform (e.g., Google Docs), Humanly
   would make me more likely to avoid using external AI tools when they are not
   allowed by the task rules.
3. From a writer's perspective (e.g., student), the certificate would help me
   prove the authenticity of my content creation when sharing it publicly.
4. From a writer's perspective (e.g., student), I would feel anxious knowing
   that Humanly records my writing behavior in fine-grained detail while I
   write.

## Verifier Survey

Survey link:
https://app.deliberation.io/participate/a8da2cb6-3a99-4101-9c21-447675546b16

Setup: participants receive background context in Deliberation.io and inspect
fixed Humanly certificate screenshots to answer the following questions.

### Activity Log

1. [Usability] The activity log provides a comprehensive view of the writer's
   process, including typing, pasted content, AI-assisted actions, focus or
   selection events, timestamps, and editing patterns.
2. [Role Perception] From a verifier's perspective (e.g., professor or
   instructor), the activity log would help me make a fairer judgment than
   reviewing only the final submitted text (e.g., PDF/Doc only).

### Certificate Seal

1. [Usability] The certificate seal helps me understand whether the certificate
   record has been verified as unchanged since it was issued.
2. [Role Perception] From a verifier's perspective (e.g., professor or
   instructor), the certificate seal makes the certificate feel more trustworthy
   when I review it.

### Authorship Statistics

1. [Usability] The authorship statistics help me understand how the final text
   was composed from human typing, pasted text, and AI assistance.
2. [Role Perception] From a public verifier's perspective (e.g., audience
   member), I would find this process-based certificate more trustworthy than a
   final-text-only AI detector score (e.g., "90% probability AI-created / 10%
   probability human-created") for judging whether a text was written by a
   human, AI, or mixed.

### Replay

1. [Usability] The replay feature and its controls are easy to understand.

### Abnormal Behavior Review

1. [Usability] After seeing the Abnormal Behavior Review examples, this feature
   helps me identify writing behaviors that may need closer attention.
2. [Role Perception] From a verifier's perspective (e.g., professor or
   instructor), the Abnormal Behavior Review makes potential policy-risk
   behaviors more visible, helping me decide whether a writing process needs
   closer review.

### Environment Setting

1. [Usability] The environment section clearly explains the writing policy and
   settings used for this task.
