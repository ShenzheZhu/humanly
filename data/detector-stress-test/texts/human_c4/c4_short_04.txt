Small milestone, but I'm allowing myself to celebrate it: I just crossed 20,000 words on my current project. For context, this is further than I've ever gotten before. Every previous attempt stalled somewhere around the 5,000-word mark, usually because I'd lose faith in the idea and start something new. This time I made one rule for myself, which was simply not to stop, even when the writing felt bad. And a lot of it does feel bad. But it turns out momentum matters more than quality in a first draft, at least for me. There's something quietly powerful about watching the word count climb day after day. It makes the finish line feel real instead of theoretical. I still have a long way to go, and I know the hard work of revision is waiting, but for today I just wanted to share the win with people who understand why it matters. Onward.
