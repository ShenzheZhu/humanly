Lately I've been wrestling with something uncomfortable: the more I study craft, the harder it is to actually write. I used to draft freely, without second-guessing every sentence. Now I catch myself flagging "weak verbs" or "head-hopping" before I've finished a paragraph, and the joy drains out of it. I don't regret learning the rules, but I think I internalized them as judgments rather than tools. There's a real tension between developing skill and preserving the messy, instinctive part of creativity that made me want to write in the first place. I'm trying to remind myself that drafting and editing are different modes, and that the rules are supposed to serve the work, not strangle it. Has anyone else gone through this? How did you get back to writing for the love of it without unlearning everything you'd studied? I'd appreciate any perspective, because right now the blank page feels heavier than it used to.
