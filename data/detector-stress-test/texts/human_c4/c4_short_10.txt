A year ago I self-published a niche nonfiction book about a hobby I love, and I wanted to share an honest reflection on how it went. The short version: it did far better than I expected in its small corner of the market, even reaching the top of its category for a while, and it brought in a modest few thousand dollars over the year. The longer version is more nuanced. That figure won't replace a salary, and the hours I poured into writing, editing, and marketing would make the hourly rate laughable. But that was never really the point. The book reached readers who genuinely cared about the subject, and the messages from people who found it useful have meant more than the income. If you're considering self-publishing something specialized, my honest take is to manage your financial expectations while valuing the other rewards. The reach and credibility it builds in a community can be worth far more than the sales.
