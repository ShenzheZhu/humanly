I tried a writing exercise that sounds odd, but it completely transformed my understanding of my own story, so I have to share it. Basically, I stepped back and looked at my plot the way an outside reader might, hunting for hidden patterns and connections I never consciously planned. I started asking "what if this detail secretly mattered?" and treating my own draft like a puzzle to be theorized about. To my surprise, this surfaced themes and links I'd been circling unconsciously the whole time. Subplots that felt disconnected suddenly clicked into a larger design once I gave myself permission to read into them. It was like discovering the book already knew what it was about before I did. I'd recommend this to anyone feeling stuck or sensing their story lacks cohesion. Approaching your work as a curious reader rather than a stressed author can reveal structure you didn't realize you'd built. It genuinely felt like uncovering a goldmine.
