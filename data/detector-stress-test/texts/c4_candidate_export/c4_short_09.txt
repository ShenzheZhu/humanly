I almost don't believe it myself, but after barely writing anything for two years, I've produced over 30,000 words in the past few days. For a long time I'd convinced myself I'd lost it, that whatever drive once made me a writer had quietly disappeared. I'd open a document, stare at it, and close it again. Then something shifted this week. An idea grabbed me and wouldn't let go, and suddenly the words were pouring out faster than I could shape them. I'm a little wary of the crash that sometimes follows a burst like this, so I'm trying not to over-romanticize it. But I also want to honor the fact that the well wasn't dry after all. If you're in a long dry spell and wondering whether you'll ever write again, take this as a small sign that the spark can return when you least expect it. Sometimes you just need the right idea to wake it up.
