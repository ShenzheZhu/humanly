I want to share something that genuinely changed how I write. For years I was paralyzed by an ambitious "important" project, the kind of sprawling epic I thought would prove I was a real writer. I barely produced anything because every sentence felt like it had to be perfect. Then, almost as a joke, I started writing something low-stakes and a little silly, based on a goofy premise nobody would take seriously. And suddenly the words came. No pressure, no expectations, just play. I learned more about pacing, dialogue, and finishing in those casual pages than in years of agonizing over my "masterpiece." The lesson I took away is that lowering the stakes can be the thing that finally lets you build momentum and skill. The serious project is still there, and I'll get to it. But I'm a much better writer now because I gave myself permission to write something that didn't matter. Highly recommend trying it.
