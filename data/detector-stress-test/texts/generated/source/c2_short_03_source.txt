I studied creative writing at University alongside English literature. Each week we'd do a writer's workshop and whatnot, show our work around, writing to some task we had been set the week before.
Problem was, being a literature course first and foremost, the majority of the tutors were fixated on literary fiction; they'd always be questioning the meanings, the symbolism, the point of everything we wrote. And God forbid if you wrote something without a deeper meaning.
The problem is now, that after graduating, I can't write a damn thing. Any idea I come up with, there's a voice in my mind saying "why? What's the point? What's the message you're trying to get across here?" and it stops me stone-dead.
I even tried writing some short stories in different genres to try and get going, but I couldn't. Even though I wasn't trying to write literary fiction, each time the same road block would appear and stop me in my tracks.
Sure, I don't have a problem writing literary fiction now and again, but it's damn hard work and I just want to enjoy writing the way I used to, without putting too much meta-thought into it.
Has anyone else ever had similar issues, and how did you cope with it?
