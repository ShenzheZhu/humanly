About a year ago I self-published a nonfiction book about my attempt to seriously improve at a niche sport. It was a fun project, part memoir and part practical guide, and I honestly thought the audience would be small but enthusiastic.

In one sense, it “worked.” The book hit #1 in its Amazon category a few times and has stayed near the top of some very specific bestseller lists. I’ve had nice emails from readers, a few blog mentions, and some encouraging reviews. From the outside, it probably looks like a successful little book.

Financially, though, it has been much more modest than I expected. Across paperback, ebook, and audio, it has made only a few thousand dollars in a year. I’m grateful for that, but it’s nowhere near enough to justify the time if I look at it purely as a business decision.

I’m curious how other nonfiction writers think about this. Is niche authority enough, or is the real challenge finding a market that is actually willing to buy?
