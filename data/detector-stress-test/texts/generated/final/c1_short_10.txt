Hello everyone.
Last year I self-published a book about an attempt I made to get really good at playing table tennis.
It did 'well' in that it topped some of the Amazon best-seller lists and remained near the top for the rest of the year. It's currently still #1 on the USA Amazon kindle table tennis bestseller list.
But despite that sales haven't been great.
In the last year it has sold:
Paperback: 443 copies sold - £1,665.39 (~$2,200)
Kindle: 429 copies sold - £1,003.46 (~$1,400)
Audiobook: 82 copies sold - £405.57 (~$550)
To be honest, I thought there would be a lot more demand. I got pretty excited when I saw the best-seller tag and soon deflated after checking the sales figures.
To the more experienced writers, how does this compare with your experiences?
If you're interested in a more detailed breakdown of how the year went you can check out my post on it.
