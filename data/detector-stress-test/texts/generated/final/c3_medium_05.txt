This learning project offers the possibility of learning the fundamentals of today’s video games, as well as the main techniques for creating and designing them, along with some tools for developing them that are used in the professional environment of the interactive entertainment industry. The emphasis is placed on training participants in game design, at the mechanical, dynamic, and aesthetic levels, integrating audiovisual resources with the programming of behaviors, etc., in order to create a quality work.

In addition, participants are encouraged to put what they have learned into practice through the development of a series of practical exercises in the form of small games that illustrate all the principles and processes seen in the theory, and that allow them to begin entering the profession of video game designer.

Teaching materials The main teaching materials of this project are the lessons that are developed collaboratively among all participants. These lessons, and in general any activity in this learning project, may have associated recommended readings: bibliographic sources, guides, articles from Wikipedia itself or from Wikibooks... and of course references to video games. The most relevant supplementary materials for the whole learning project are collected in the references section of this same page.

Although it has a certain theoretical component, this learning project includes practical activities. Exercises, practical cases, knowledge-level tests, group projects, or similar tasks are welcome, all of them activities that must be associated with their respective lessons.

There is a class calendar with the real dates of the university master’s course taken by the “first participants,” which mark the beginning of this learning project.
