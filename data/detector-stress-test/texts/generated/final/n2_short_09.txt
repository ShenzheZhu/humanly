I don’t really have anyone in my day-to-day life who would understand why this feels so big, so I’m putting it here.

For the last couple of years, my manuscript has mostly just been an icon on my desktop that made me feel guilty whenever I saw it. Every now and then I’d open it, read a paragraph, decide it was all terrible, and close it again. Work, school, bills, relationships, burnout — all of it became an easy excuse not to try.

On Monday night, I opened it for “just ten minutes,” mostly to prove to myself that I didn’t care about it anymore. Instead, I started remembering why I began writing it in the first place. I fixed one scene, wrote another, and then stayed up far too late.

It’s Thursday now, and I’ve added a little over 18,000 words.

I know this kind of momentum can disappear, and I’m trying not to treat it like magic. But for the first time in ages, writing feels alive again, not like homework from some past version of me.
