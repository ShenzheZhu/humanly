Objectives This learning project offers an overview that will familiarize the learner with the subject of study in such a way that he or she identifies and defines its basic concepts; recognizes the most important authors and moments in the development of the discipline; can establish and classify the relevant topics of corporate image and its relationship with other fields; distinguishes the methodologies proper to its study, so that finally he or she may estimate the importance of the contribution of corporate image in broadening the understanding of organizations today.

Corporate image has to do with the perception that the publics have of an organization. Paul Capriotti, author of Corporate Branding, describes it as the set of “attributes that the publics associate with a company.” The corporate image sector is therefore in charge of managing these attributes of the identity of organizations and how they are communicated to the publics. It is through the correct management of the image that the organization becomes known to the publics, or in Capriotti’s words, “through corporate image the organization exists for the publics.”

When we speak about corporate image, it is necessary to explain in detail that corporate image has various attributes, which give value to the corporate image of an organization. It is important to determine the attributes on which a public builds the corporate image. Each public values different attributes in an organization. The attributes of corporate image are divided into current attributes and latent attributes, and the current attributes in turn are divided into main attributes and secondary attributes. The main attributes are those that a public expects to see in an organization, while the secondary attributes are those that the organization offers to its public but that are not specifically expected by the public. The main attributes are classified into basic attributes and discriminatory attributes. The basic attributes are those that describe what a public expects from an organization, that is, these attributes describe the “minimum basic requirement” demanded by the public. Meanwhile, the discriminatory attributes are those that the organization uses in order to differentiate itself from another organization. In conclusion, current attributes are those that at this moment make up the corporate image of an organization, while latent attributes are those that at this moment are not forming the corporate image of an organization, but that in the future may do so.
