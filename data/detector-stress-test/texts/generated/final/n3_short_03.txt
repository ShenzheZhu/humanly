I used to take great joy in writing. If I thought of a scene, a strange person, or an absurd ending, I would immediately open a document and rush forward. Later, after taking writing classes for several years, every assignment came with questions: What does this image represent? What does the character desire? What are you trying to critique? Has the theme developed? I know these questions are not wrong; in fact, they are often very useful. But now they feel like a row of teachers sitting in my head, staring at every sentence I write and giving it a grade.

The worst part is that I no longer know how to “just write.” As soon as I have an idea, I begin judging it: too shallow, too vulgar, meaningless, not literary enough. In the end, I cannot even write a single page. Maybe the old me wrote terribly, but at least I was writing. Now I understand many techniques, yet all I can do is delete the opening.

Has anyone else experienced this? How do you get back that urge to write without needing to prove yourself?
