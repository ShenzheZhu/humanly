I really need to shout this here: my novel is finally revised!!!

From that messy document at the very beginning to the version I can now honestly call “a complete book,” almost three years have passed. In that time, I overturned the main plot, cut chapters I could hardly bear to let go of, and turned a character who was originally just passing through into a central figure. More than once, I stared at the screen and thought, forget it, this thing can’t be saved.

But today, after rereading the final chapter, I suddenly realized it really does stand on its own now. The characters are no longer just speaking for me; they have their own choices, mistakes, and voices. The passages I once worked hardest on are now the cleanest cuts, while a few little details I added casually have become my favorite parts.

Next will probably be proofreading, organizing submission materials, and maybe facing more rejection letters. But tonight, I don’t want to be anxious. Tonight, I just want to let myself be happy: I wrote it, and I revised it.
