This may sound a little melodramatic, but I was so stuck on my plot that I started questioning everything, and a strange method saved it: I pretended I wasn’t the author, but the kind of reader who writes three-thousand-word conspiracy theories on forums.

I reread the main storyline from the beginning and asked myself irritating questions: What if this character is lying? What if that “coincidence” wasn’t one? What if the motive the protagonist believes in is only the surface? The more I thought about it, the more wrong it felt, until I found a hidden thread I could add without overturning the plot. Many parts that had seemed too smooth and direct suddenly gained a second layer of meaning.

The surprising thing is that I’d worried the story was just “event A leads to event B,” but now the relationships and worldbuilding feel more charged. Not every story needs a huge twist or mastermind, but looking at your draft through a reader’s overinterpretation can reveal spaces you’ve missed. If you’re stuck, try it.
