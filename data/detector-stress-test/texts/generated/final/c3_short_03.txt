I have several times experienced a situation where someone uses a sentence with “bis auf,” and it is not clear to me whether an inclusive or exclusive meaning is intended. For example, at a doctor’s: “Please undress bis auf your underwear.” In this case, should the underwear be taken off, or may it be kept on? “I ate the pastries bis auf the last cookie.” Were all the cookies eaten here, or is there still one left? “He embarrassed himself bis auf the bones.” Were the bones also embarrassed, or were they the only thing not embarrassed? Duden recognizes both uses: I have the impression that the inclusive sentence becomes an exclusive one if I move the parts of the sentence around. Does the meaning generally depend on the word order? Can a rule be formulated for when the exclusive meaning is intended and when the inclusive one is?
