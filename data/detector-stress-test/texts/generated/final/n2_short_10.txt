About a year ago, I self-published a nonfiction book about trying to get seriously better at a niche sport. It was a fun project, part memoir and part practical guide, and I expected the audience to be small but genuinely interested.

In some ways, the book did well. It reached #1 in its Amazon category a few times and has stayed near the top of a few very specific bestseller lists. Readers have sent kind emails, a few blogs have mentioned it, and the reviews have been encouraging. From the outside, it probably looks like a small success.

The money has been a different story. Across paperback, ebook, and audio, it has earned only a few thousand dollars in a year. I’m grateful for that, but if I judge it purely as a business decision, it doesn’t come close to justifying the time I put into it.

I’m wondering how other nonfiction writers think about this. Is niche authority enough, or is the harder part finding a market that is actually willing to buy?
