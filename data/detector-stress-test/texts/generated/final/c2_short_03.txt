I studied creative writing at university alongside English literature. Each week we had a writers’ workshop, sharing work written to a prompt we’d been given the week before.

The problem was that, because it was mainly a literature course, most tutors were fixated on literary fiction. They always questioned the meanings, symbolism, and point of everything we wrote. God forbid you wrote something without a deeper meaning.

Now, after graduating, I can’t write a damn thing. Any idea brings a voice saying, “Why? What’s the point? What message are you trying to get across?” It stops me stone-dead.

I’ve tried short stories in different genres to get going, but the same roadblock appears every time. I don’t mind writing literary fiction now and again, but it’s hard work, and I just want to enjoy writing the way I used to, without so much meta-thought.

Has anyone else had similar issues, and how did you cope?

Edit: Huge thanks to everyone. Your feedback is helping and making me want to write again. I’d reply to every comment if I could.
