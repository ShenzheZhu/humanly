I used to write all the time: bad fantasy scenes, strange little horror stories, pages of dialogue that led nowhere. Most of it probably wasn’t good, but it felt alive.

Then I started taking writing seriously. Workshops, craft books, critique groups, the whole thing. Now, whenever I sit down, I can’t get through a first paragraph without picking it apart. Is the narrator’s voice clear enough? Is there conflict? Is the image earning its place? What does the setting mean? Am I leaning on cliché? Is the theme too obvious, or worse, missing completely?

I know those questions matter at some point. But they’ve crept into the drafting stage and started acting like bouncers. Nothing gets through unless it can prove it has literary value.

I miss the way I wrote before I knew what I was supposed to be doing. I don’t want to be ignorant again. I just want the joy back.
