I’ve gone almost two years without writing seriously. The novel file kept sitting on my hard drive; whenever I remembered it, I felt guilty, then immediately found an excuse to close it: too busy, not in the right mood, the old writing was too childish, and no one would read it anyway. Then, before leaving work this Tuesday, I somehow opened it and read a few chapters. I only meant to mock my past self, but instead I was pulled back in.

At lunch I secretly added a few passages, and after getting home I wrote until midnight. The next few days were the same: thinking through the plot while commuting, noting dialogue during meals, and being unable to stop once I sat down at night. Just now I checked the count: I’ve added more than 30,000 words in these few days.

I know this may just be a sudden burst, not proof that I’ve really “recovered.” But I finally want to know what happens next, and writing no longer feels like a burden. I hope I can keep this momentum and not disappear for another two years.
