Only recently did I realize that what really got me writing again was not some grander worldbuilding concept, nor revising the outline until it was perfect, but temporarily setting aside the project I felt “had to become my masterpiece.”

I had been thinking about that story for years. The maps, races, history, and magic system were all piled up in great detail, but every time I opened the document, it felt as if I were turning in an answer sheet for my entire life. After two sentences, I wanted to delete them. Later, I played a game with friends whose setting was extremely casual but genuinely fun. Its backstory was full of holes, and that actually took the pressure off. I started writing little scenes for our characters, mission logs, fake legends, and even some very stupid dialogue.

Strangely, these unimportant things helped me rebuild the habit of finishing a passage, sharing it, and then writing the next one. No one expected it to be great, and I did not need to prove anything. I still want to return to that big project, but at least I understand this now: sometimes writing something easy and low-risk is far more useful than continuing to stare at a dream in a daze.
