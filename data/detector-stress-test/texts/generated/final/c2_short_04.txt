This is my first Reddit post, but I felt like celebrating. I’ve been “working” on a novel for years, but kept getting stuck trying to make the first draft “perfect.” I could never bring myself to fix things in post, worried the story itself would be unfixable.

This year, I finally buckled down and aimed for at least 200 words a day. At first, it felt like walking on burning coals each night. Eventually, it became a habit, then something I ache to do every day—even during the day, which I never used to do.

I’m still in the first act, so the book could stretch on for a while. But I thought the average novel was around 40,000 words. Tonight, when I hit 20,000, I had to sit back, think about how long it took, and play Bon Jovi’s “Livin’ on a Prayer.”

Maybe it can inspire blocked writers like I was.
