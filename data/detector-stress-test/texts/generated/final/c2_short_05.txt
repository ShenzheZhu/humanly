For years, I worked on an ambitious sci-fi/fantasy project. I guess I still am; I definitely want to finish it. But I couldn’t write it regularly. The writer’s block was constant, perhaps because of the pressure I was putting on myself.

I solved the problem by accident. I played League of Legends, a video game with truly derpy lore that still had some cartoony charm. I started a community for roleplaying and themed matches, and wrote filler fanfic/fanlore to add flavor to the competitions. People enjoyed it, so I kept going. It was easy because my attitude was, “Whatever, just write something. It doesn’t have to be perfect; it’s just for a silly game.”

Over time, I found myself almost unconsciously trying to make that throwaway lore good. More than a year in, I realized I was writing regularly. It was very Zen: I only succeeded when I “gave up.”

This is just a start, but after years of procrastination and angst, I’m pleased I’ve begun in earnest. Like so many real authors say: you’ve just got to write.
