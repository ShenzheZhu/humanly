This may sound a little ridiculous, but I stumbled into a useful plotting exercise by pretending my book already had an obsessive fandom.

I took a scene that seemed straightforward and asked, “If people online were trying to prove this meant something else, what would they point to?” Then I tried the same thing with character motives, repeated details, strange timing, unanswered questions—everything. Instead of treating my outline as the final word, I treated it like evidence.

Somehow, it helped. A side character’s odd choices started to suggest a stronger hidden agenda. A few scenes I’d thought of as simple connective tissue began to look like possible foreshadowing. Even the parts that felt too neat gave me places to add tension without changing what actually happened.

I don’t think every story needs a huge secret conspiracy. But reading my draft like a suspicious fan made it feel less flat. If your plot feels too simple, try misreading it on purpose.
