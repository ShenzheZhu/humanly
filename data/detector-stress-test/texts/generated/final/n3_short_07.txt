I just got a call from my agent, and my hands are still shaking. Nothing is officially settled yet, and the contract won’t be signed until next Monday, so I can’t say which publisher it is, and I can’t tell my parents, coworkers, or friends in real life. But I really can’t keep it in anymore, so I’m coming here to shout it out: I might be getting a book published.

I’m not the kind of writer who had an easy path from childhood onward. I only started writing seriously in my thirties. I worked during the day and revised at night. After finishing my first book, I felt pretty good about it; looking back, it was almost unbearable. With the second, I learned structure, but wrote characters as stiff as wood. With the third, someone was finally willing to read it to the end, but it still fell short. I was rejected so often I went numb, and I wondered if it was too late, if I should put away the “writer dream.”

I still don’t dare celebrate too early. The contract isn’t signed, and there will be revisions, promotion, and figuring out real-life work. But tonight, I want to let myself be happy for a moment. Thank you to everyone here who has shared experience, rejection letters, and encouragement. You truly helped someone silently lurking.
