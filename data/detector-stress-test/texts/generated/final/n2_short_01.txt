I just typed “The End” on the first draft of my first novel, and I’m still staring at the screen like it might vanish if I blink.

I’m fifteen. I started it last year because I had a cool idea and no real understanding of pacing, character arcs, or how many times a person can “smirk” in one chapter before it becomes a crime. It’s a fantasy/sci-fi mess, full of dramatic speeches, a villain whose motivation changes every hundred pages, and at least three plot twists I’m pretty sure don’t make sense.

But it’s finished. It has a beginning, a middle, and an end. About 90,000 words of probably terrible writing that I kept coming back to anyway.

I know editing comes next, but I don’t know how long to leave it alone, what to look for when I start, or whether I should show it to anyone yet. If you’ve finished a first draft, what did you do next?
