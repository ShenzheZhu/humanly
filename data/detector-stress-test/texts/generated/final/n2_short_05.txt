For years, I thought “being a writer” meant steadily working on the huge novel I’d been carrying around in my head since college. It had maps, religions, political factions, three magic systems, and not a single finished chapter. Every time I opened the document, I felt like I was stepping into a cathedral with muddy shoes.

Then, almost by accident, I started writing short stories for a silly multiplayer game my friends and I were obsessed with. The official lore was absurd, full of contradictions, and clearly not built to support anything serious. That ended up being exactly what I needed. There was no pressure to be profound. I could write a scene in an evening, post it, make people laugh, and move on.

It showed me that finishing small, imperfect things builds a kind of strength that planning a masterpiece never did. I still care about the big project, but I no longer treat it like something sacred. I’m just trying to write.
