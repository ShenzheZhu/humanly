I recently read that the German word Waage has only officially been written with a double a since 1927 (a quotation from the 1929 Duden). This change can be nicely visualized with the help of the Google Ngram Viewer. I then tried to find a few more examples. In doing so, I noticed that in German the o is only rarely lengthened with a double vowel (“Moor”), and that the letter u, as far as I know, is not doubled at all. The long i exists as the diphthong ie. The letters a and e are found relatively often as double vowels. So my question is: why are some words lengthened with a double vowel, while others are not, or are lengthened with a silent h? And why is the distribution so different for the individual vowels?
