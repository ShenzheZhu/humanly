About a year ago I self-published a nonfiction book about my attempt to seriously improve at a niche sport. It was a fun project, part memoir and part practical guide, and I was honestly expecting a small but enthusiastic audience.


In one sense, it “worked.” The book hit #1 in its Amazon category a few times and has stayed near the top of some very specific bestseller lists. I’ve had nice emails from readers, a few blog mentions, and some encouraging reviews. From the outside, it probably looks like a little success.


Financially, though, the results have been a bit disappointing. Across paperback, ebook, and audio, it has made only a few thousand dollars in a year. I’m grateful for that, but it was far from justifying the time, looking at it from a business perspective.


I’m curious how other nonfiction writers think about this. Is niche authority enough, or is the real challenge finding a market that is actually willing to buy?
